Daily AI Routines That Actually Work

I’ve tried a bunch of AI tools, but the ones that stick are the simple ones. Morning brainstorming with a prompt, midday edits to refine ideas, and evening check-ins to clear mental clutter. It’s not about being perfect—it’s about showing up, even when the chaos feels louder than the calm.

Why Cosmic Dinner Feels Like a Creative Ritual

Cooking these dishes isn’t just about food. Teff injera bowls, gimbap, palak paneer—they all bring structure to my day. Recipes become routines, and routines keep my creative energy from burning out. The seasons shape what I cook, just like they shape what I create.

Balancing Tech and Soul Without Losing Yourself

AI helps, but it doesn’t replace the messy parts of making art. I’m still figuring out how to talk about creativity without sounding like a buzzword factory. The key is honesty—letting the tools do the heavy lifting while I focus on the human bits that matter.

It’s not about perfection—it’s about showing up, even when the chaos feels louder than the calm.
